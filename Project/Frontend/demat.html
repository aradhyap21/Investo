Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Demat Account?

A Demat (Dematerialized) Account is an account that holds your securities (shares, bonds, mutual funds) in electronic form, similar to how a bank account holds your money. It eliminates the need for physical certificates and makes trading and managing investments much easier and safer.

What documents are needed to open a Demat account?

You need the following documents to open a Demat account:

  • PAN Card (mandatory)
  • Address Proof (Aadhaar/Passport/Driving License)
  • Identity Proof (if different from address proof)
  • Bank Account Details with canceled cheque
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Income proof (in some cases)
What are the charges involved in maintaining a Demat account?

Common charges associated with a Demat account include:

  • Account Opening Fees (one-time)
  • Annual Maintenance Charges (AMC)
  • Transaction Fees (when buying or selling securities)
  • Brokerage Charges (varies by broker)
  • Dematerialization Charges (for converting physical shares)
  • Rematerialization Charges (for converting electronic shares to physical)

These charges vary by broker and service provider. Some brokers offer zero account opening fees or waive AMC under certain conditions.

Can I have multiple Demat accounts?

Yes, you can have multiple Demat accounts with different depository participants or brokers. There is no restriction on the number of Demat accounts you can open. However, you need to maintain each account separately and pay applicable charges for each account.

How do I transfer shares between Demat accounts?

To transfer shares between Demat accounts, you need to submit a Delivery Instruction Slip (DIS) to your depository participant. The DIS should contain details like the recipient's Demat account number, DP ID, and the number of shares to be transferred. Alternatively, many brokers now offer online transfer facilities through their platforms.
