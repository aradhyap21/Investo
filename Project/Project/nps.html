About National Pension Scheme

What is NPS?

The National Pension System (NPS) is a voluntary, long-term retirement investment plan to create a retirement corpus. It is regulated by PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority).

Tax Benefits

Get tax benefits under Section 80CCD(1) up to ₹1.5 lakh and additional deduction under Section 80CCD(1B) up to ₹50,000.

Investment Options

Choose from various investment options including equity (E), corporate bonds (C), government securities (G), and alternative investment funds (A).

Account Types

Tier I Account

Primary retirement account with tax benefits

  • Tax benefits under 80CCD
  • Mandatory retirement account
  • Partial withdrawal allowed
  • Professional fund management

Tier II Account

Voluntary savings facility with more flexibility

  • No lock-in period
  • Withdraw anytime
  • Lower charges
  • No tax benefits

Withdrawals & Payouts

Withdrawal Requests

Subscribers can make partial withdrawals from their Tier I account after 3 years of joining NPS for specific purposes like higher education, marriage of children, treatment of critical illnesses, etc. Up to 25% of the subscriber's own contribution can be withdrawn.

Annuity Details

Upon retirement, subscribers must use at least 40% of their accumulated pension corpus to purchase an annuity from an IRDA-regulated life insurance company. This annuity provides a regular pension during retirement years. Various annuity options are available including lifetime pension, pension with return of corpus, and joint life pension.

Payout Frequency

Subscribers can choose how often they receive their annuity payments based on their financial needs. Options typically include monthly, quarterly, half-yearly, and annual payouts. Monthly payouts are the most popular choice as they align with regular expense patterns during retirement.
